Last week, on a drizzly Monday evening, the chef Ivy Chen stood in the doorway of her new West Village restaurant, Forage & Fire, greeting the first guests to sample a menu composed entirely of ingredients sourced within a five-mile radius of Manhattan. Chen, who previously helmed the Michelin-starred Willow in Brooklyn, has traded the precision of imported truffles and dry-aged beef for a hyper-local experiment: ramps from Inwood Hill Park, sea lettuce harvested off Pier 40, and venison from a cull in Staten Island’s Greenbelt.
