# Daily News Report — Jeeves

## Part 1 Hook Patterns

### The Oblique Entry (preferred)
Don't name the sector or announce the topic. Enter through a specific detail:

WRONG: "Today in AI: OpenAI has released a new model that..."
RIGHT: "The model came out on a Tuesday, which is how OpenAI signals it doesn't expect anyone to make a fuss."

### The Tension Opener
Lead with a contradiction or reversal:

WRONG: "The EU is implementing new AI regulations this quarter."
RIGHT: "Brussels wrote the rulebook. Nobody mentioned that enforcement requires a regulator who has read it."

### The Specific Before the General
Name a specific thing, then zoom out to why it matters:

WRONG: "There are several developments in the AI space worth noting."
RIGHT: "Anthropic's new safety paper runs to 84 pages. The paragraph that will matter most is on page 61."

## Section Transitions
Each part should end so the next part can begin without a connective tissue sentence.
BAD transition: "Speaking of technology, let's turn now to..."
GOOD: Hard stop. Next part opens with its own specific detail.

## Information Density Target
Every sentence should either: (a) state a specific fact, (b) make a claim about the significance of that fact, or (c) provide context that makes the fact interpretable. Sentences that do none of these should be cut.

## Voice Calibration
The briefing reads like a very well-informed friend who has already read everything and is summarizing over drinks — not a newsletter, not a news anchor, not a professor. The aside is the micro-dose of personality; the rest is compressed intelligence.
